The main focus was understanding how defensive security teams monitor, detect, analyze, report, and respond to cyber threats.

## Key Concepts Learned

### Blue Team

The Blue Team is responsible for defending systems, networks, users, and data from cyber threats. Their work includes monitoring security alerts, investigating incidents, improving defenses, and helping organizations reduce risk.

### SOC Analyst Role

A SOC Analyst is responsible for reviewing alerts, identifying suspicious activity, investigating possible threats, and escalating incidents when necessary.

The SOC Analyst Level 1 role focuses heavily on:

- Alert triage
- Basic investigation
- Ticket documentation
- Escalation
- Communication
- Following playbooks and procedures

### Humans as Attack Vectors

People are often targeted by attackers because human mistakes can lead to security incidents.

Common examples include:

- Phishing emails
- Social engineering
- Weak passwords
- Credential theft
- Malicious attachments
- Fake login pages

### Systems as Attack Vectors

Attackers also target systems through vulnerabilities, misconfigurations, weak services, exposed ports, outdated software, and poor security controls.

Common examples include:

- Unpatched systems
- Misconfigured servers
- Open ports
- Weak authentication
- Vulnerable applications
- Poor access control
